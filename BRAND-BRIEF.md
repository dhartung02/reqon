# Reqon — Brand brief

*For the UX / brand designer. Goal: enough feel for the product, its personality, and its
surfaces to express it in a logo, wordmark, and identity system. Color is open — the
sections below are raw material and constraints, not prescriptions.*

---

## In one line

**Reqon is a private, AI-assisted command center for a serious job search** — it scouts roles
automatically, scores each for fit, and tracks every opportunity from first sighting to offer,
across phone, desktop, and browser, online or off.

## The 30-second version

A focused professional running a high-stakes search doesn't have a "find a job" problem — they
have a *signal* problem. Too many postings, too little time, no clean way to know which roles
are actually worth pursuing or what stage each one is at. Reqon is the operations layer for
that: it gathers opportunities from everywhere, applies a consistent fit/probability/expected-
value score so the best rise to the top, and keeps the whole pipeline honest with reminders and
status tracking. Less hustle, more judgment.

## Who it's for

A senior, discerning operator — think Principal / Director-level product, eng, or design leaders
— running a deliberate, quality-over-quantity search. Tech-fluent. Data-driven. Privacy-
conscious (the product is self-hosted and local-first; their data stays theirs). They want
*signal over noise* and *control over chaos* — not a spray-and-pray job board, not a grind.

## What it is — three surfaces, one product

- **Reqon (the app)** — an iOS app that is the command center. A "Today" view that tells you
  what to act on now; pipeline lists (Open / Applied / Interviewing / Closed) with fit scoring;
  a detail view per role with full tracking; capture from anywhere via the share sheet; an
  in-app browser that helps fill applications. Works fully offline — no backend required.
- **Reqon Clip (the plugin)** — a Chrome extension. One click clips any job posting into the
  pipeline; a small overlay badges a role's fit / score right on the job page; "mark applied"
  writes back. A thin, fast companion to the desktop browsing flow.
- **Reqon Sync (the backend)** — an optional self-hosted server. Syncs the pipeline across
  devices, runs **Reqon Scout** (an automated daily sweep of company job boards that finds,
  scores, and queues new matches), and pushes a notification when something worth seeing turns
  up. Optional by design — the app is whole without it.

## What it does — the core verbs

These are the actions the brand should feel capable of:

- **Scout** — automated reconnaissance across job boards; finds matches you'd never catch by hand.
- **Score** — the differentiator. An AI-assisted fit + interview-probability + expected-value
  model ranks and tiers every role (A / B / C), so attention goes where it pays off.
- **Capture** — grab a role from a browser, the phone share sheet, or a chat assistant, instantly.
- **Track** — a real pipeline CRM: status, dates, follow-ups, recruiter/referral, hygiene nudges.
- **Assist** — drafts personalized application answers in *your* voice; pre-fills application
  fields. (Explicitly never auto-submits — it assists judgment, it doesn't replace it.)
- **Sync & notify** — quietly keeps every device current and pings you when the scout scores a hit.

## Personality

Sharp · focused · intelligent · calm · in-control · precise · confident · discreet.

It's a **command center, not a hustle app.** Quietly powerful. Strategic, not frantic. It
respects the user's time and intelligence. The feeling is *situational awareness* — you can see
the whole field and you know exactly where to move next.

**What it is NOT** (anti-targets the mark should steer clear of):
- Not a job board (Indeed / LinkedIn) — not mass-market, not blue-and-friendly-corporate.
- Not an auto-apply bot or a "grind / 100 applications a day" hustle tool.
- Not a generic CRM or spreadsheet.
- Not playful-startup cute. The tone is operator-grade: refined, technical, assured.

## The name — gold for a wordmark

**Reqon** — pronounced **"reckon"** (REK-un). It's a deliberate triple meaning:
- **recon** — reconnaissance: scouting ahead, gathering intelligence, situational awareness.
- **req** — requisition: the job posting itself, the atomic unit the whole product revolves around.
- **reckon** — to judge / estimate: the scoring engine's confident verdict on a role.

Scout → requisition → judgment, in five letters. The **Q** is the distinctive, ownable letter —
strong, uncommon, and visually rich (its tail, its enclosed counter). It's a natural anchor for
a glyph: a radar blip, a target reticle, a scope, a ping.

## Visual raw material (explore — don't feel boxed in)

Metaphors that fit the "recon + judgment" core:
- **Radar / sonar sweep**, the ping and its blip — also a lovely nod to the notification feature.
- **Scope / reticle / crosshair / target** — finding and locking onto the right role.
- **Signal strength / bearing / compass** — score as signal; direction and confidence.
- **Periscope / vantage point / scouting ahead** — seeing the field before others do.
- **Pipeline / funnel narrowing to the few that matter** — many in, the right ones out.
- The **Q** rendered as any of the above (reticle, radar dot, scope).

Clichés to avoid: literal briefcases, magnifying glasses, hard-hats / "job site" imagery,
generic green checkmarks, anything that reads as a mainstream job board.

## Where the mark has to live (technical constraints)

Plan for a small, scalable glyph **plus** a wordmark — both needed:
- iOS **app icon** (rounded square, full color, works at small sizes).
- **Browser-extension icon** at 16 / 48 / 128 px — must stay legible and recognizable at **16px**.
- **Monochrome** menu-bar / status glyph and push-notification icon.
- Favicon.
- A **sub-brand family**: one parent mark (Reqon) that gracefully extends to **Reqon Sync**,
  **Reqon Clip**, **Reqon Scout** — so the glyph should support labeled variants without redesign.

## Current aesthetic (continuity reference)

The existing product UI is a **dark "command-center"** interface — restrained, modern, technical
but refined (a Linear / Vercel sensibility), currently pairing a serif display face (Fraunces)
with a clean sans (Spline Sans). Treat this as the current state to honor or evolve, not a
mandate — but the new identity should feel at home on a dark, precise, data-forward surface.
